What Is Herbal Medicine, Exactly?

Plants are used to make herbal medications. Plant materials such as leaves, petals, and roots are used. Each plant component can be used medicinally in a different way.

Several approaches are used to extract compounds from plant components. They make the treatment from fresh and dried plants.

The purpose of herbal medicine is to restore your body's ability to defend, regulate, and heal itself.

It is a comprehensive strategy that considers your physical, mental, and emotional well-being. Phytomedicine, phytotherapy, and herbal medicine are additional terms for the same thing.

Manufacturers make many drugs from plants. On the other hand, herbalists do not extract plant components in the same manner that pharmaceutical companies do.

Herbalists believe that the treatment works because the complete plant or plant combinations have a precise chemical equilibrium. Not even from a single active component.

Western herbal medicine and Chinese herbal medicine are the two most common types of herbal medication used in the United States. Tibetan and Ayurvedic medications are two less common types of drugs.
